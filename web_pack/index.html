RULES OF THE CLUB

THE CLUB

The name of the club is Chess Club ('the Club').

The Club is a proprietary private membership restaurant, bar and club, owned and operated by XXX Ltd, ADDRESS. Registered Company Number: XXX. The Proprietor reserves the right to charge members a joining fee and an annual subscription fee. The right to amend or waive any such fees or subscriptions shall be in the Proprietor’s sole discretion.

OBJECTIVE

The objective of the Club is to provide food, beverage and recreational facilities for its members and members’ guests.

MEMBERSHIP COMMITTEE

The Membership Committee is appointed by the Proprietor. The Membership Committee has the right to refuse any application for membership without giving reason. The Membership Committee will not enter into any correspondence in relation to fees.

MEMBERSHIP

Membership is open to people over 18 years of age on a non-discriminatory basis. The applicant will submit an application form online. Every candidate must be proposed by a member and seconded by another member; the proposing member will be asked to vouch for the candidate from their personal knowledge via an e-mail of written support. Membership is non transferable.  Any inaccuracy in or omission from an application may render it void at the discretion of the Membership Committee.

JOINT MEMBERSHIP

Joint Membership is available to married couples and those in civil partnerships. If the couple ceases to be married or their civil partnership ends then each member will be charged for memberships fees at the full individual rate the following year. Joint members are jointly liable for membership fees and charges for food and beverages purchased at the Club.

OVERSEAS MEMBERSHIP

Overseas Membership is available to people who spend more than nine months per year abroad with their primary address overseas.

UNDER 30 MEMBERSHIP

Under 30 Membership is available to successful applicants aged between 18 and 30 years old. After your 30th birthday, annual membership fees will be adjusted.

FEES

The annual subscription fee and the joining fee are determined by the Proprietor. Upon approval of your membership, your membership fees are payable immediately. By providing us with your credit card details and/or bank details for Direct Debit, you accept and consent to being charged with membership fees upon your application being approved or renewed. Cheques are not accepted as payment for fees.

Membership is for a minimum of one year and renewable thereafter on an annual basis. If you have selected monthly payment and choose to resign your membership or your membership is cancelled part way through the year, it is at the discretion of the management whether you have to pay for the remainder of the year.

All membership fees are to be paid to Chess Club and never to any other party.

Failure to pay the annual subscription within one month of the due date after being notified by management will result in membership being terminated.

PROPOSING NEW MEMBERS

We invite current members to propose new applicants to join the Club. The application form is available online at chessclublondon.com. The Membership Committee meets biannually and admits new members as space becomes available. A valid form of identification is required to verify eligibility for membership.

The Club shall use all reasonable endeavors to ensure that no applicant, member or guest receives less favourable treatment on the grounds of their sex, race, religion or belief, disability, marital status, age, religion, sexual orientation, nationality, colour or ethnic origin.

MEMBERSHIP CARDS

Membership Cards must be presented to the Reception on entry. Any member who allows his or her card to be used by a non-member will have their membership terminated.

GUESTS

Members are permitted up to four guests. A member’s guests may not enter the club without the member present. Members must not sit separately to their guests or allow their guests to remain in the Club when they leave. Members are responsible for ensuring their guests follow all club rules and policies, and can face suspension or termination of membership if their guests violate those rules. The guest of a member must sign and write his/her name in the book supplied for that purpose. Former members who have been expelled and members who are under suspension may not be admitted as guests to the Club.

BOOKINGS/RESERVATIONS

Reservations can be made by telephone or e-mail but must be cancelled or amended by telephone. Failure to fulfill a reservation may result in a cancellation fee determined by the Proprietor.

ANIMALS

We welcome dogs in the Club if they are house trained and well behaved. Please be considerate of other members if you bring a dog to the Club and keep it under your supervision at all times.

CHILDREN

We welcome children in the Club if they are house trained and well behaved. Please be considerate of other members when you bring children to the Club and keep them under your supervision at all times.

ATTIRE

There is no dress code at Chess Club however, members and your guests are asked to maintain an acceptable standard of dress.

MOBILE PHONES/LAPTOPS

Mobile phones and laptops are allowed throughout the Club; however, please be considerate of other members when using them. Films, videos and music should only be played through headphones and must not be audible to other members.

STAFF

The appointment and dismissal of all employees is at the sole discretion of the Proprietor.
Members and their guests are expected to conduct themselves in a courteous manner with the Club’s employees.

PRIVATE PARTIES

We may occasionally close the Club to members for private events. Please contact XXX if you are interested in hiring any of the spaces.

CAMERAS AND RECORDING DEVICES

No cameras, video or recording devices may be used while in the Club. The Club reserves the right to confiscate recording devices and any photos, videos or other recorded images used or taken while on Club premises.

PRIVACY FOR MEMBERS/GUESTS

Members and their guests must not approach or solicit other people in the Club who they do not know personally. Doing so may lead to expulsion from the Club.

PRESS/SOCIAL MEDIA

The Club is a strictly press and social media free zone. Members will be expelled from the Club, and potentially have their membership suspended or terminated, if they or their guests communicate information to the press or on social media regarding other members, guests, or Club affairs.

CONFIDENTIALITY

The Club guarantees full confidentiality regarding the records and personal information of its members, except in cases of legitimate police inquiry.

BILLS AND CREDIT

There are credit facilities available at Chess Club – member’s who have given a direct debit instruction to the Club may choose to make a payment for food and drink this way although the Club reserves the right to ask for bills to be settled immediately. The direct debit will be drawn shortly after the charges are incurred. The Club may limit the sizes of these charges. Members are responsible for any unpaid bills of their guests.

MEMBERSHIP RENEWAL

Membership renewals are not automatic, but reviewed annually by Membership Committee. The decision on renewal is final and cannot be appealed.

RESIGNATION

Members may resign their membership at any time. Refund of any unredeemed portion of the annual membership fee is at the discretion of the management.

EXPULSION OF A MEMBER

The following list of behaviours will result in the immediate expulsion from Club premises of those responsible, along with any accompanying members or guests, and may result in suspension or termination of membership:

  • Any form of discrimination, harassment or bullying towards any other member, guest or employee of the Club
  • Violent, abusive or intimidating behaviour
  • Serious insubordination and misconduct under the influence of alcohol
  • Deliberate removal, damage or destruction of property belonging to the Club, its members or its guests
  • Any disclosure to the press or social media of information regarding other members, guests or Club affairs
  • Betting or gambling for money in the Club
  • Ingesting, possessing or distributing illegal drugs on Club premises
  • Any conduct likely to endanger the Club’s Premises License
  • Any conduct, either inside or outside the house, likely to damage the Club’s reputation

When a member who has been expelled is allowed to return to Club premises is at the discretion of the management.

TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP

Termination of membership is ultimately at the discretion of the management. The decision is final and cannot be appealed.

Those who have had their membership terminated will not be refunded the unredeemed portion of their annual membership fee and cannot return to the Club as a guest in future.

RIGHTS AND PRIVILEGES OF MEMBERSHIP

All members are entitled to the full rights and privileges of the Club, but have no proprietary rights with regard to Club premises or anything found therein.

DISSOLUTION

The Club may be dissolved by the Proprietor at any time.

SMOKING

No smoking is allowed inside the Club at any time. All members and their guests are obligated to abide by all applicable no-smoking governmental laws.

CODE OF CONDUCT AND LICENSING CONDITIONS

Members and their guests are asked to be mindful of the residents on Chesterfield Street and to enter and leave the Club quietly. Disturbances by members or their guests on the street may result in termination of membership.
Sale or supply of alcohol in the Club is permitted within the hours and conditions permitted under the Premises License in respect of the Club premises.

LIABILITY OF THE PROPRIETOR

All property brought into the Club by any member or their guest shall be at their sole risk and neither the Proprietor nor any employee of the Club shall be liable for any loss or damage to any such property.
The Proprietor, its employees, and agents shall not be liable to any member or guest of a member for any injury suffered by them or their property. However, nothing in these Rules and Regulations shall be deemed to exclude the Club’s liability for death and/or personal injury arising out of any negligent or unlawful act by the Club.

DISPUTE OF THE RULES

In the event of any dispute arising as to the meaning or interpretation of these rules, the matter shall be referred to the Membership Committee, whose decision shall be final.
These rules shall be governed and construed in accordance with English law and you agree to submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England.

NOTICES

The Proprietor may alter or revoke the rules of the Club at any time. Such alterations shall be deemed to have been brought to the notice of members provided that a copy is conspicuously displayed on the Club’s official website www.chessclublondon.com

CCTV
